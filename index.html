Uralo-Austronesian Languages

Uralo-Austronesian is a proposed language familiy suggested by George van Driem in 2015. This language familiy spoken in Eurasia, Madagascar, Oceania and northern North America.

The branches of Uralo-Austronesian proposal include Uralic, Altaic (Turkic, Mongolic, Koreanic, Japonic and Tungusic), Ibero-Caucasian (Kartvelian, Nakh-Dagestanian and Abkhazo-Circassian), Dravidian, Sino-Tibetan, Wuyic (Hmong-Mien and Austroasiatic), Austro-Tai (Kra-Dai and Austronesian), Chukotko-Kamchatkan-Amuric (Chukotko-Kamchatkan and Nivkh), Yukaghir and Eskimo-Aleut. In addition, the language isolates of Basque and Ainu are included. This proposal of Uralo-Austronesian would be one of macrofamilies, such as Indo-European and Afroasiatic.
